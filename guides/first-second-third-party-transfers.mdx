---
title: "Party Types (1st, 2nd, and 3rd Party Transfers)"
---

**Purpose:** define party types (1st/2nd/3rd), when to use **client account** vs **managed accounts**, and how **addresses** map to each party.

### Quick definitions

- **Client account**: your Brale account (KYB’d). It owns the API key and is the top-level container.
- **Managed account**: a sub-entity under the client account that represents _your end customer_ (your customer’s customer).
- **Address**: a destination or source for value movement. Addresses can be **internal** (Brale-custodied wallets tied to an account) or **external** (bank accounts or non-custodial on-chain wallets associated to an account).

### Party types (what “party” means)

A “party type” tells you **who is the principal** in the transfer (whose transaction it is), and whether Brale is acting **for itself** or **on behalf of** a client.

#### 1st party

Brale ↔ Brale. You will not use this for typical integrations.

#### 2nd party

Client ↔ Brale. The transfer is between Brale and the client (direct customer relationship).

#### 3rd party

End customer (managed account) ↔ someone else, _with the client initiating on their behalf_.

### When to use client account vs managed accounts

Use this decision rule:

- Use the **client account** when the transfer is for the **client’s own treasury** (the client is the principal).
- Use a **managed account** when the transfer is for the client’s **end customer** (the end customer is the principal), especially when:
  - the end customer needs their own internal custodial wallets
  - you need to track activity per end customer
  - you need “who is this transfer for?” to be explicit in your system

### How addresses map to parties

Addresses always belong to (are created under / associated with) either the **client account** or a **managed account**.

| Party type | Principal (who it’s “for”) | Account used                                                                      | Typical address ownership                                                      | Typical use                               |
| :--------- | :------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------- |
| 1st party  | Brale                      | N/A (Brale internal)                                                              | Brale-owned bank + wallets                                                     | Brale treasury operations                 |
| 2nd party  | Client                     | Client account                                                                    | Client’s internal wallets + client’s external bank/wallet addresses            | Client mints/redeems/swaps for themselves |
| 3rd party  | End customer               | Managed account (recommended) or client account with explicit branding (advanced) | End customer internal wallets; external recipient addresses (bank or on-chain) | Payouts/collections for end users         |

### Worked examples

#### Example A — 2nd party onramp (client mints for themselves)

Scenario: the client funds a mint using their own bank rail and receives stablecoin to their own wallet.

- **Party:** 2nd party
- **Account to use:** client account
- **Addresses:** client external bank address (source) → client internal wallet (destination)

#### Example B — 2nd party offramp (client redeems for themselves)

Scenario: the client redeems stablecoin and receives USD to their own bank.

- **Party:** 2nd party
- **Account to use:** client account
- **Addresses:** client internal wallet (source) → client external bank address (destination)

#### Example C — 3rd party on-chain payout (client pays an end user)

Scenario: the client pays a recipient wallet that belongs to an end customer.

- **Party:** 3rd party
- **Account to use:** managed account (end customer)
- **Addresses:** managed account internal wallet (source) → external on-chain address (destination)

#### Example D — 3rd party ACH payout with branding

Scenario: the client initiates an ACH payout to an end recipient bank account and wants the client’s name shown on statement.

- **Party:** 3rd party
- **Account to use:** managed account (end customer) for tracking, plus branding rules as applicable
- **Addresses:** managed account internal wallet (source) → external bank address (destination)

### Common pitfalls

- If you are paying **someone other than the client**, it’s almost always **3rd party**.
- If you cannot answer “who is this transfer for?” from the resource model, you likely need **managed accounts**.
- Treat “branded” payouts as **3rd party** by default.
