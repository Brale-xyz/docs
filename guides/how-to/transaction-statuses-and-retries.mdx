### What this guide is for

If you are building on Brale, you will occasionally see a transaction that does not complete on the first attempt.

This guide explains:

- What transaction statuses mean, including what is terminal vs non-terminal.
- How Brale automatic retries work.
- When and how to request assistance.

### Key ideas

- A transaction can enter a `failed` status even when the overall outcome is not final. `failed` is often a _non-terminal_ state.
- Some failures are automatically retried. Others require investigation or manual handling.
- If automatic retries do not succeed, manual intervention may be required.

---

### Statuses

> The exact set of statuses and fields may vary by product surface and endpoint. This page focuses on how to interpret the most commonly observed states.

#### `complete`

- **Meaning:** The transaction succeeded.
- **What you should do:** Nothing.

#### `canceled`

- **Meaning:** The transaction will not be complete. This is a terminal outcome.
- **What you should do:**
  - Treat this as final for your system.
  - If you expected the transaction to complete, contact support with the information in Reporting / requesting assistance (below).

#### `failed`

- **Meaning:** An attempt to process the transaction did not succeed.
  - **Important:** `failed` is _not necessarily terminal_. A transaction in `failed` often later transitions to `canceled` or `complete` after retries and or manual processing.[[1]](https://linear.app/brale/issue/1da92cc3-c06b-45ac-a59f-9d5d38635ef5)
- **What you should do:**
  - If your integration is polling, continue to poll until the transaction reaches a terminal outcome.
  - If your integration is event-driven, handle `failed` as an intermediate state and continue listening for later updates.
  - If the transaction remains `failed` after a reasonable amount of time, follow the instructions in Reporting / requesting assistance (below).

### Automatic retries

Some failures are retryable. If the failure reason is known to be retryable, Brale automatically retries the transaction on supported chains.

- **Retry limit:** Brale attempts automatic retries up to **15 times**. After that point, the transaction typically requires manual intervention.

> Note: Retry eligibility is based on the underlying failure reason, not just the `failed` status.

---

### How to build resilient integrations

#### 1) Do not treat `failed` as final

Design your state machine so that `failed` can later become `complete` or `canceled`.

#### 2) Use idempotency (recommended)

When you re-submit operations on your side (for example, after timeouts), ensure your requests are idempotent to avoid duplicates.

#### 3) Handle delayed completion

If your product experience needs a “final answer” quickly, consider:

- Displaying a “processing” state until terminal.
- Allowing users to refresh or check back.

---

### Reporting / requesting assistance

If a transaction appears stuck (for example, it remains `failed` and is not progressing), Brale may need to investigate.

When contacting Brale support, include:

- Transaction ID (and or order ID, if applicable)
- Approximate timestamp of creation (with timezone)
- Chain (if relevant)
- Any visible error messages
- Whether this is blocking an end-user flow

### FAQ

#### Are retryable `failed` transactions retried indefinitely?

No. Brale automatically retries up to **15** times. After that, manual intervention is typically required.

#### Which chains have auto-retry?

Auto-retry is supported for EVM and Solana chains (for retryable reasons).
